Adding a New ConsultantSIMTEC THERAPEUTIC

Follow these steps in order every time you give a consultant access to the system. Step 3 is the one people forget — without it, their login exists but the system doesn't know who they are.

1

Add them as a consultant first

Go to Admin → Consultants & Teams and add the consultant. Note the exact spelling of the name you use — you'll need it to match, character for character, in step 3.

Why it matters: the consultant record is what their sales, commissions and diary all hang off. The login is added on top of it, not instead of it.
2

Create their login in Supabase

In the Supabase dashboard: Authentication → Users → Add user.

  • Enter their email address.
  • Set a temporary password — a different one for each person, never a shared password.
  • Tick Auto Confirm User (otherwise they can't sign in until they click an email link).

When the user appears in the list, click them and copy their UUID (the long id). You need it next.

Never share one password between consultants. If everyone uses the same login you can't tell who did what, and when someone leaves you'd have to reset it for the whole team.
3

Link the login to the consultant (the step people forget)

Creating the login does not tell the system who they are. Run this in Supabase → SQL Editor → New query, filling in the three highlighted values:

-- Paste the UUID from step 2, their email, and their name.
-- consultant_name MUST match the name in Consultants & Teams exactly.
insert into profiles (id, email, full_name, role, consultant_name, active)
values (
  'PASTE-UUID-HERE',
  'their.email@simtectp.com',
  'Their Full Name',
  'consultant',
  'Their Full Name',   -- must match exactly
  true
);
The name must match exactly. A stray space or a different spelling and their diary will refuse them with "Your login isn't linked to a consultant record." Copy and paste the name rather than retyping it.
4

Check it worked — before you tell them the password

Run this. Every consultant must show YES ✓.

select coalesce(string_agg(
  p.consultant_name||' → role='||p.role
  ||' | linked to consultant record: '||
  case when exists(select 1 from commission_consultants cc
       where lower(trim(cc.name))=lower(trim(p.consultant_name))) then 'YES ✓' else 'NO ✗' end,
  E'\n' order by p.consultant_name),'none')
from profiles p where p.role='consultant' and p.active;

Anyone showing NO ✗ has a name mismatch. Fix the spelling in profiles.consultant_name so it matches commission_consultants.name, then re-run the check.

Do this before handing over the password. It takes ten seconds and saves a confused phone call from someone standing on a doorstep.
5

Hand it over

Give them their email address and temporary password, and tell them to:

  • Sign in at the system address, then
  • Change their password immediately (Reset password screen).

They'll land on My Sales, with the gold 📅 My Diary button in the top corner.

What they can and can't see. A consultant sees only their own sales, their own customers and their own diary. They cannot see other consultants' figures, the pay runs, debt recovery, product prices or commission rates. That's enforced by the database, not just by hiding buttons.
6

When someone leaves

Don't delete them — their sales, commissions and history must stay intact. Instead:

-- Revokes access immediately. History is preserved.
update profiles set active = false
where consultant_name = 'Their Full Name';

Then in Supabase → Authentication → Users, delete or ban the user so the password no longer works.

Setting active = false is what locks them out — the system checks it on every page load and signs them straight back out.
!

Two standing rules

One password per person, always. Turn on leaked-password protection in Supabase → Authentication → Policies. It blocks passwords known from public data breaches.
Every consultant is a logged-in user of the database. The only thing separating them from customer records, pay runs and debt recovery is Row Level Security. Any new table must have RLS enabled and a policy written before it holds real data. No exceptions.
